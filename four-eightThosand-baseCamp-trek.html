Description

Embark on an extraordinary adventure with our exclusive trekking package, an epic journey that unveils the base camps of Pakistan's four legendary 8000-meter peaks: K2, Gasherbrum I and Gasherbrum II. At K2 Adventure, we invite you to join us on this unparalleled odyssey, where each step brings you closer to the heart of the world's mightiest mountains.

HightLights

  • Begin your trekking odyssey at the foot of K2, the second-highest peak on the planet

  • Traverse through the captivating landscapes of the Karakoram Range to reach the iconic K2 Base Camp.

  • Venture into the Gasherbrum Massif, trekking to the base camp of the mighty Gasherbrum I.

  • Continue your journey to the base camp of Gasherbrum II, the world's 13th highest mountain.

  • Marvel at the breathtaking views of the Gasherbrum Range and soak in the serene atmosphere of the high-altitude wilderness.

  • Experience the thrill of standing in the shadow of this formidable peak, surrounded by pristine alpine beauty.

Travel Plans

Day-1 : Arrive at Islamabad International Airport, Islamabad +

After pickup, you’ll be transferred to our partner hotel to relax and recover from your journey.

Day-2 : Fly to Skardu +

Participants traveling this morning will enjoy a stunning flight from Islamabad to Skardu, with a chance to see Nanga Parbat, weather permitting. Upon arrival in Skardu, the day is yours to explore at your leisure.

Day-3 : Spend free day in Skardu +

After breakfast, participants will hike to Kharpocho Fort, perched on a mountain with breathtaking views of the Indus River and Skardu town. You’ll also have the option to visit Sadpara Lake, followed by free time for shopping in Skardu Bazaar. While you enjoy the sights, our staff will handle the necessary government documentation, which may take up to two days. We’ve built in contingency days at the end of the trek to accommodate any delays.

Day-4 : Drive to Askole village +

Participants will embark on a jeep safari to Daso, traveling through the stunning Shigar Valley alongside the Braldu River, which extends to Askole. Askole marks the last village on our route, where participants will start their trek after leaving the jeeps behind. Depending on road conditions, you may begin your walk before reaching Askole. The journey will take approximately six to eight hours to complete.

Day-5 : Trek from Askole to Jhola via the snout of Biafo Glacier +

After finalizing the necessary supplies and equipment, the porters will prepare for the trek to Jhola. This valley serves as the gateway to the Biafo Glacier, which flows over fifty kilometers from Hispar La to Braldu. Participants will navigate the moraine as they cross the glacier's snout and descend to the lunch spot beside the Korofon River.

The route will lead us to the confluence of the Braldu and Domordo rivers. Instead of the older trail, we’ll take a new path upstream, crossing the river via a bridge. After crossing, participants will descend to the edge of the Braldu River and follow the riverbank to reach the camp at Jhola.

Day-6 : Trek from Jhola to Paiju following the Braldu river +

On this day of the K2 Base Camp trek, participants will follow the Braldu Gorge along the riverbank, enjoying stunning views of Trango Tower and Cathedral Peak. To avoid sunburn, it's important to stay protected, as conditions can be dry and hot.

Along the way, several glacier-fed streams may require river crossing shoes, depending on water levels. On a clear day, you’ll spot the snout of Baltoro Glacier. The day will conclude with a climb away from the river to reach the camp at Paiju, the last campsite with trees for a while.

Day-7 : Acclimatize and rest in Paiju +

Participants will spend the day at Paiju Camp for acclimatization and rest. Meanwhile, the porters will bake bread for the upcoming week on the glacier. For those interested, an acclimatization walk to the base camp of Paiju Peak is also available.

Day-8 : Trek from Paiju to Khoburtse +

On this day, the trek will be challenging, but the scenery will become increasingly breathtaking as you approach the snout of Baltoro Glacier. The trail features continuous up and down climbs over the glacial moraine; while it's generally safe, participants should remain cautious at all times.

Day-9 : Cross small glaciers and walk to Urdukus +

Trango Towers can be eventually seen after coming across two small glaciers. For the following night the participants will make their stop at Urdukas near the army camp. The camp is 100m above the glacier.

Day-10 : Trek to Goro II, the junction of the Baltoro glaciers and Young husband +

On this day of the K2 Base Camp trek, participants will walk through the heart of the glacier, following the medial moraine. To the south, you'll enjoy views of Masherbrum (7821m) as you navigate the rocky moraine, passing impressive ice seracs along the way.

Tonight, you'll camp for the first time on Baltoro Glacier at Goro II, where the Baltoro and Younghusband glaciers meet. Be prepared for a significant drop in temperature, with stunning views of Masherbrum and Muztagh Tower unfolding before you.

Day-11 : Trek to Concordia through a rocky moraine +

On this day of the K2 Base Camp trek, participants will reach Concordia after a continuous ascent along the moraine. As you walk, more high snow-capped peaks will come into view, culminating in your first sighting of K2. Other notable peaks visible include Broad Peak, Mitre Peak, Gasherbrum, Sia Kangri, and many more.

Day-12 : Trek to Broad Peak BC, Gilkey Memorial and K2 Base Camp +

Today, we’ll trek up the Godwin-Austen Glacier to the Art Gilkey Memorial, dedicated to those lost on K2. We'll start early, crossing glaciers along a faint trail that leads us closer to K2, which grows more majestic with each step.

This day is reserved for any potential delays if the domestic flight didn't operate the previous day. Guests will depart early from Chilas to Islamabad, a journey that can take up to ten hours.
